Research

ICML 2026

Adaptive Termination in Agentic Multimodal RAG via Cross-Model Evidence Grounding

International Conference on Machine Learning — ~26% global acceptance rate — Published as a first-year undergraduate

Most retrieval-augmented systems retrieve greedily or stop arbitrarily — they don't know when they know enough. This paper introduces two contributions: CMEGS (Cross-Model Evidence Grounding Score), a claim-level grounding metric that measures whether retrieved evidence actually supports each assertion; and MIR (Minimum Information Requirement), a principled stopping criterion that gives a multimodal agentic RAG pipeline a mathematically grounded sense of sufficiency.
